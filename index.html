2⚡ Tesla charging strategy (whole trip)

There is no Supercharger in Port Angeles and none at Sol Duc / Lake Crescent. Your three Superchargers are Sequim, Forks, Aberdeen — all 8-stall, ~150–250 kW.

SuperchargerAddressWhen you use it
⚡ Sequim1441 E Washington St, Sequim · 8 stallsDay 1 charge-up. Top to ~90–100% on the way in — it's your last fast charge before two days in the park.
⚡ Forks100 Fern Hill Rd, Forks · 8 stallsDay 3 buffer + optional Day 2. ~35 min from Sol Duc. Quick top-up right after leaving camp; or a Day-2 safety top-up if Hurricane Ridge drained the battery.
⚡ Aberdeen701 W Wishkah St, Aberdeen · 8 stalls (Safeway)Day 3 main charge. Midpoint of the drive home — charge + lunch/dinner + restroom reset.
⚡ OlympiaOlympia (I-5)Day 3 optional. Only if range feels tight or I-5 traffic is heavy.
The simple rule: ⚡ Sequim → (park) → Forks → Aberdeen → home. Charge to 100% at Sequim Day 1 so the no-charging stretch (Sol Duc + Hurricane Ridge round-trip) is covered with margin. Cold mountain mornings + the climb to Hurricane Ridge use extra range — if you dip lower than you like on Jul 4, the Forks Supercharger is a stress-free 35-min hop from camp. Tip: precondition the battery before the Hurricane Ridge climb; you'll regen most of it back on the descent.

😌 Plan C — Anyone melting down: skip the Ridge, stay near camp

Lowest-stress, near-zero driving. Save Hurricane Ridge for "next time" and let Sol Duc + Lake Crescent carry the day.

  • Morning: Sol Duc Falls (1.6 mi) or the short Ancient Groves nature loop right by camp.
  • Midday: hot-springs soak → camp lunch → nap.
  • Afternoon: Lake Crescent — Devil's Punchbowl swim / kayak or paddleboard rental at the lodge.
  • Evening: second soak, July 4 campfire + s'mores + glow sticks.
Bottom line: early or late, you win — both skip the Hurricane Ridge reservation and the July-4 mid-morning crush. Only the "normal" 7:30 start needs a timed-entry ticket, so if you didn't snag one, just lean into Plan A, B, or C.

Do just: ⚡ Forks top-up → Ruby Beach (20 m) → Kalaloch (main, 1 h) → ⚡ Aberdeen (charge + dinner) → home. Skipping Hoh + Quinault gets you home closer to ~6:30–7 pm.

6🎒 Camp & packing notes

Camp essentials

  • Tent, sleeping bags/pads, headlamps, camp chairs, lantern
  • Layers + rain shells (coast + rainforest are wet/cool even in July)
  • Warm hats/gloves for Hurricane Ridge (30–40°F cooler up top)
  • Water shoes + towels for hot springs & Lake Crescent swim
  • Bug spray (Sol Duc valley), sunscreen, first-aid kit
  • Bear-aware food storage — use the campsite locker; never food in the tent

Kid + EV notes

  • Child carrier for dirt trails (Sol Duc Falls, Hoh); stroller for paved stops
  • Cooler with kid snacks — limited food past Forks on Day 3
  • Download offline Google Maps — no cell signal in much of the park
  • Charge to 100% at Sequim Day 1; treat Forks as your in-park safety net
  • Marshmallows + glow sticks for the July 4 campfire (no fireworks in park)
